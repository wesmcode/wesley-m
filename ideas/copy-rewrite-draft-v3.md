## What v3 adds on top of v2

A third positioning lane: **platform & AI modernization for established companies**. This is the reimagined ThoughtWorks consulting work — same methodology vocabulary (evolutionary architecture, strangler fig, fitness functions, lean inception), delivered now as a single-practitioner advisory engagement instead of a 6–15 person embedded team.

The wedge, sourced from the modernization research:
- Tier 1 firms (ThoughtWorks, Accenture, Capgemini) sell modernization as tech-led, $500K+, 9–12 months.
- Boutiques deliver comparable outcomes in 6–8 weeks at $150K–$300K.
- McKinsey's own data: 88% of enterprises use AI, only 6% see EBIT impact. The gap is execution.
- 68% of enterprises bought Copilot licenses they can't deploy. 67% of AI projects never reach production.
- The credibility test in 2026: "Show me your production systems." Wesley can answer; a McKinsey team can't.

Wesley's three-layer credential for this lane:
1. **ThoughtWorks 2019–2023** — trained in evolutionary architecture, strangler fig, fitness functions, lean inception (Caroli Lean Inception Facilitator cert).
2. **Accenture 2014–2019** — five years as software engineer on platform programs in financial and professional services.
3. **Code and Theory 2025** — shipped eight production AI agents and the V0→Payload migration call. The "production systems" answer to the credibility test.

The thesis line (lifted from the research synthesis, lightly edited):
> Trained in the methodology where it was invented. Engineered the systems where they were the size of the problem. Shipped the agents when the tools became real. The mid-market buyer needs all three; no Tier 1 firm sells them at a price that fits.

### FAQ — 6 questions, two of them adjusted for the enterprise lane

**How do you scope a project before we commit?**
> A 30-minute call, then a 1-page proposal: scope, deliverable, price, timeline, and the three assumptions I'm making. If the assumptions are wrong, say so and I redo the page. No commitment until both sides sign it. Enterprise buyers — happy to deliver against an SOW with defined deliverable gates.

**How do you price?**
> Audits, sprints, OKR resets, modernization assessments, and AI readiness work are fixed price. Migrations scope per system. Hourly advisory is a flat weekly rate. Phase-2 advisory retainers (modernization or fractional) are monthly with a 3-month minimum. Every number lands in the written proposal before you commit.

**Can you start this month?**
> Audits and advisory, usually yes. Sprints, modernization assessments, and migrations depend on the calendar — I keep one deep engagement at a time. Ask and I'll tell you what's open.

**How do you work alongside our existing PM and engineering team?**
> As a peer. Your PM stays the owner; I bring outside eyes and the unhurried hours they don't have. Every deliverable gets a real handover with a read-out session so the team owns it after I'm gone.

**What's your AI/LLM experience?**
> Senior PM on the multi-agent SaaS AI platform at Code and Theory — 8 agents shipped in 90 days, 4 enterprise pilots signed, $120K+ pipeline. I resolved the ML team's 90%+ confidence-threshold standoff with the design team using a transparency-first approach the enterprise buyer could defend.
