#### Founder-led shapes

**Card 1 — Product audit** *3–4 weeks · Fixed price · Founder or enterprise*
> Three weeks. I walk the funnel, interview 20 churned customers, sit through sprint planning, and hand you a Flash Report: top 3 systemic blockers, one symbolic quick win, 90-day fix list. The deliverable goes straight to engineering, not into a slide deck.

**Card 2 — Discovery sprint** *4–6 weeks · Fixed price · Founder-led*
> Customer interviews and synthesis. Jobs-to-be-done framing. An opportunity-solution tree. One north-star bet written in a sentence. You leave knowing what to build and which features the data already says to retire.

**Card 3 — OKR & roadmap reset** *4–6 weeks · Fixed price · Founder-led*
> Quarterly planning workshop, north-star metric, OKRs that survive the first sprint. Output: a roadmap doc your engineering lead doesn't argue with and a stakeholder deck that doesn't need a rerun.

#### Enterprise / modernization shapes

**Card 4 — Platform modernization assessment** *4–8 weeks · Fixed price · Mid-market*
> A working diagnostic, not a slide deck. Application portfolio inventory, retirement-vs-migrate calls, a phased plan where each phase has standalone business value. I worked the PM side of platform programs at ThoughtWorks (2020–2023) and led the recent five-subsite consolidation at Code and Theory into a 65M-views flagship with 65 DA held through cutover. Single practitioner, advisory shape — no six-person consultancy team.

**Card 5 — AI readiness & pilot roadmap** *4–8 weeks · Fixed price · Mid-market*
> A use-case audit against your existing AI investments (Copilot, Einstein, ServiceNow AI, or a custom build). Build / buy / apply framework. Governance gaps mapped against the same questions enterprise procurement is asking. Pilot-to-production roadmap for the highest-value, lowest-risk use cases. Informed by shipping production agents at Code and Theory, not by a strategy deck.

**Card 6 — Multi-CMS / headless migration** *Scoped per system · Founder or enterprise*
> CMS consolidation and multi-site migration with SEO held through cutover. Five subsites into a 65M-views flagship is the recent reference. CMS-agnostic: Payload, Contentful, Liferay DXP, Drupal. Headless migration where it earns its place.

**Card 7 — Hourly advisory** *Pay as you go · Either side*

### Who I work with — 5 buyer cards

**Founder-led teams, no PM yet.** Series A or pre-A. Engineering is 7–12 people. The founder has been doing product and is becoming the bottleneck. Two days a week of senior product cover until the full-time hire lands.

**AI-curious teams without production LLM experience.** You want to add AI features. Nobody on the team has shipped one. You need someone who has navigated multi-agent orchestration, confidence thresholds, and the messy conversation about what an AI feature actually costs to run.

**Mid-market teams covering a leave.** A PM is on parental leave or has moved on. Recruiting takes 3–9 months. I pick up the sprint cadence next week.

**Growth-stage teams stuck shipping the wrong things.** Release velocity is fine. Adoption is flat. Nobody owns the north-star metric. Three weeks of audit; a 90-day reset.

**Mid-market companies in the AI execution gap.** You've already paid for the strategy work — Copilot or Einstein licenses, a roadmap from a Tier 1 firm. None of it is in production yet. You don't want another 12-month consultancy engagement. You need a senior practitioner with shipped systems who can write the phased plan your CFO will sign.
