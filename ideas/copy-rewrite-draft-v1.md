## Positioning thesis

Wesley is a **fractional product manager with deep AI/LLM production experience**. The two strongest signals for buyers right now are:

1. He has shipped LLM features into production (8 agents at Code and Theory, 4 enterprise pilots in 90 days). The research is explicit: the AI fractional niche is the highest-value segment in 2025–2026, and most fractional PMs don't have shipped LLM features. (Source: Smith Horn, Lenny's/Aman Khan, Reforge.)
2. He has run multi-property CMS migrations with revenue on the line — a second specialty that very few fractional PMs offer.

Around those two pillars: 6+ years of PM craft (discovery, OKRs, roadmap, mentoring), a five-year engineering background that earns him credibility with eng leads, and EN/PT fluency that opens LATAM and Iberia.

Voice rules used throughout: short sentences. Concrete number wherever a noun would do. Em-dash budget = 12 site-wide. No "X, not Y" cliché. No "quietly". No "honest". No "the bar". No "in practice".

### FAQ

Cut from eight questions to six. Tighter answers.

**How do you scope a project before we commit?**
> A thirty-minute call, then a one-page proposal: scope, deliverable, price, timeline, and the three assumptions I'm making. If the assumptions are wrong, say so and I redo the page. No commitment until both sides sign it.

**How do you price?**
> Audits, sprints, and OKR resets are fixed price. Migrations scope per system because they vary. Hourly advisory is a flat rate with weekly office hours. Fractional retainers are monthly, three-month minimum. The price is on the proposal before you commit.

**Can you start this month?**
> Audits and advisory usually yes. Sprints and migrations depend on the calendar — I keep one deep engagement at a time. Ask and I'll tell you what's open.

**How do you work alongside our existing PM and engineering team?**
> As a peer, not above. Your PM stays the owner; I bring outside eyes and the unhurried hours they don't have. Every deliverable gets a real handover with a read-out session so the team owns it after I'm gone.

**What's your AI/LLM experience?**
> Eight production AI agents shipped at Code and Theory in ninety days. Multi-agent orchestration, RAG, eval harnesses, prompt versioning, inference-cost models, and the call to kill an agent that wasn't earning. I've also killed AI features that demoed well — sometimes that's the value.

**Do you sign NDAs?**
