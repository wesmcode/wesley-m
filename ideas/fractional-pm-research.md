## 1. Definition — What "Fractional" Actually Means

### The cleanest working definition

A fractional product manager is a senior PM who embeds part-time — typically 10–20 hours per week — into a single company's team, owns a real scope (roadmap, discovery process, team rituals), and delivers against outcomes rather than just producing recommendations. The key differentiator from adjacent roles: accountability without full-time employment.

Source: [Mind the Product — "What is fractional product management?"](https://www.mindtheproduct.com/what-is-fractional-product-management/)

> "A fractional product manager is not a consultant, not an advisor, not a temporary hire — they operate as embedded leadership."

---

### Fractional vs. the adjacent roles

| Role | Time commitment | Duration | Accountability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fractional PM** | 10–20h/week per client | 3–9+ months, rolling retainer | Owns roadmap, leads team cross-functionally |
| **Interim PM** | Full-time (40h/week) | 3–12 months, defined end date | Covers a gap or transition |
| **Advisory/Consultant PM** | Episodic, on-demand | Project or hourly | Recommends; does not execute |
| **Embedded contractor** | Usually full-time | Through a staffing agency | Executes specs, does not own strategy |

The sharpest contrast is fractional vs. consultant: **consultants recommend, fractional PMs deliver.** A fractional PM takes ownership, drives execution, and is accountable for results within the organization.

Source: [LogRocket — "What does a fractional product manager do?"](https://blog.logrocket.com/product-management/fractional-product-manager-role/)

---

### What "fractional" looks like in practice

- **% of week:** Typically 25–50% of a working week per engagement (10–20 hours).
- **Number of clients:** 2–4 simultaneously is common. Rohit Katiyar explicitly caps at 3–4 clients to maintain quality. Source: [rohitkatiyar.pro — Fractional Product Manager](https://rohitkatiyar.pro/fractional-product-manager)
- **Contract length:** Minimum 3–6 months. 6-month minimum is common on published pricing pages. Engagements that stick tend to run 9–18 months.
- **How many fractionals are there?** The number of LinkedIn profiles mentioning fractional leadership rose from 2,000 in 2022 to 110,000 in 2024. Source: [Fractional Officer — Top 2025 Trends](https://www.fractionalofficer.com/top-2025-trends-fractional-executive-leadership-solopreneur)

## 2. Engagement Shapes

These are the actual service packages practitioners sell, with approximate durations and price bands.

---

### Shape 1: Product Audit / Flash Assessment
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks  
**Deliverable:** A "Flash Report" — top 3 systemic blockers, one symbolic quick win, retention curve / activation funnel analysis, competitive gap summary.  
**Typical price band:** Fixed fee, ~$5,000–$15,000 depending on scope.

The Umbrex Fractional CPO Playbook describes it this way: the fractional leader runs a "rapid product field scan covering retention curves, activation funnels, feature-adoption stats, support ticket heat maps, and competitive gaps" while conducting stakeholder interviews. By end of week two: a Flash Report.  
Source: [Umbrex — Fractional CPO Playbook](https://umbrex.com/resources/fractional-executive-playbook/fractional-chief-product-officer-playbook/)

---

### Shape 2: Discovery Sprint / Strategy Engagement
**Duration:** 6–12 weeks  
**Deliverable:** Vision Canvas, strategic pillars, outcome-based OKRs, roadmap with prioritized bets, customer interview synthesis.  
**Typical price band:** $10,000–$30,000 fixed, or absorbed into an early retainer.

The Umbrex playbook specifies milestones: Vision Canvas + strategic pillars by Day 15; outcome-based OKRs adopted by Day 45; discovery repository and auto-refresh dashboards by Day 90.

---

### Shape 3: Ongoing Embedded Fractional (Core Product)
**Duration:** 3–12 months, rolling  
**Commitment:** 10–20 hours/week  
**Deliverable:** Continuous: sprint alignment, roadmap governance, weekly triage, quarterly OKR reviews, PM coaching for the team.  
**Typical price band:**

| Provider | Hours/week | Monthly rate |
|---|---|---|
| The Fractional Product Manager | 10h | $7,000/month |
| The Fractional Product Manager | 20h | $14,000/month |
| Rohit Katiyar (<$1M ARR) | ~10h | $3,000+/month |
| Rohit Katiyar ($1–10M ARR) | ~10h | $4,000+/month |
| Rohit Katiyar (>$10M ARR) | ~10h | $5,000+/month |
| Smith Horn Group (AI-first teams) | 10–20h | $60K–$120K/year ($5K–$10K/month) |

Sources:
- [The Fractional Product Manager — Pricing](https://thefractionalproductmanager.com/pricing/)
- [rohitkatiyar.pro](https://rohitkatiyar.pro/fractional-product-manager)
- [Smith Horn Group — Fractional Product Leadership for AI-First Teams](https://www.smithhorn.ca/services/fractional-product-leadership)

For context: a full-time senior PM in the US costs $120,000–$180,000 annually plus benefits (early-stage) to $200,000–$300,000+ all-in (growth stage). Fractional delivers comparable senior expertise at roughly 30–50% of that cost.

---

### Shape 4: AI/LLM Product Sprint
**Duration:** 4–8 weeks  
**Deliverable:** AI opportunity map, prioritized roadmap of "what to automate first / what to skip," initial eval harness design, prompt versioning workflow, inference cost model per feature.  
**Price band:** Emerging category, often $15,000–$40,000 fixed or a premium retainer.

The Smith Horn Group specifically positions fractional product leadership for "AI-first software teams," embedding 10–20 hours per week alongside engineering to ship, not just advise.  
Source: [Smith Horn Group](https://www.smithhorn.ca/services/fractional-product-leadership)

---

### Shape 5: Parental Leave / Interim Cover (Fractional)
**Duration:** 3–12 months, full coverage  
**Commitment:** Varies — often 20h/week for continuity  
**Deliverable:** Continuity of roadmap ownership, stakeholder relationships, sprint ceremonies.  
**Price band:** Closer to interim PM pricing ($8,000–$15,000/month), often higher than standard fractional because it functions more like interim cover.

Product People explicitly markets this: "Fractional PM services that cover parental leaves for 3–12 months or unstaffed permanent roles while hiring takes 3–9 months. Seasoned PMs (5+ years experience) who start within 3–10 days."  
Source: [Product People — Fractional Product Manager](https://www.getproductpeople.com/service/fractional-product-manager)

Mother Cover is an agency specifically for this use case: "fractional and interim support for parental leave, manager-level and above."  
Source: [Mother Cover Agency](https://www.mothercoveragency.com/)

---

### Shape 6: OKR / Roadmap Reset
**Duration:** 4–8 weeks  
**Deliverable:** North-star metric proposal, OKR set for next two quarters, roadmap doc with rationale, stakeholder alignment deck.  
**Price band:** Often bundled into a discovery engagement or standalone $8,000–$20,000.

## 3. Buyer Signals — Who Hires and When

These are the actual trigger situations, synthesized from primary sources.

---

### Trigger 1: Series A startup with no PM yet
The founder has been doing product, the engineering team is 7–12 people, and decision-making is a bottleneck. They can't afford a $200K senior hire yet, but they need one. A fractional PM at $4,000–$7,000/month bridges to the full-time hire.

Lenny Rachitsky's guidance (consistent across his newsletter): hire your first PM when you've hit PMF, your engineering team exceeds 7 people, and you're ready to delegate roadmap ownership — typically right after Series A. A fractional PM can start this process earlier without the commitment risk.  
Source: [Lenny's Newsletter — Joining as the first product manager](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/joining-as-the-first-product-manager)

---

### Trigger 2: Mid-market company covering a departure or leave
A PM goes on parental leave. Recruiting a full-time replacement takes 3–9 months. The team needs continuity now. This is the cleanest ROI case for fractional.

---

### Trigger 3: AI-curious team that has never shipped an LLM feature
A product team wants to add AI features but nobody on the team has shipped one in production. They need someone who has navigated evals, prompt versioning, cost ceilings, model drift, and the "kill the feature" decision. This is the highest-value fractional niche in 2025–2026.

---

### Trigger 4: Founders who need a second opinion
Pre-seed or seed-stage founder who has been making all product decisions alone. Not ready for a PM hire. Wants 4 hours a week of structured pushback from someone senior.

---

### Trigger 5: Growth-stage company where the product org is a feature factory
The team ships a lot but outcomes are flat. Adoption is weak. Nobody owns the north-star metric. Leadership brings in a fractional PM to diagnose and reset the operating model. Lisa Hagen (BPMA) describes this trigger: "The product team is busy — releases are happening — but adoption is flat and key business metrics aren't improving." The fractional leader "quickly identifies whether the issue is weak product strategy, missing customer insight, or unclear success metrics."  
Source: [BPMA / Lisa Hagen — 5 Problems a Fractional Product Leader Can Help You Solve](https://www.bostonproducts.org/blog/5-problems-a-fractional-product-leaders-can-help-solve)

---

### Trigger 6: PE/VC portfolio company needing execution acceleration
Private equity and VC firms increasingly place fractional PMs in portfolio companies post-acquisition or post-investment to compress time-to-value. Prodify documents a case where a fractional CPO drove 4x product usage increase in 8 months.  
Source: [Prodify — How I Drove 4X Product Usage as a Fractional CPO](https://www.prodify.group/blog/fractional-product-executive-4x-usage)

## 4. Common Deliverables

What a fractional PM actually hands over (concrete artifacts):

| Artifact | Description |
|---|---|
| **Product Audit / Flash Report** | Systemic blockers, activation/retention analysis, quick win, competitive gaps |
| **PRD (lean)** | Problem statement, rationale, hypotheses, acceptance criteria — not 40-page specs |
| **Vision Canvas** | Strategic pillars, multi-year direction tied to business model |
| **Opportunity Solution Tree** | Teresa Torres framework: outcome → opportunities → solutions → experiments |
| **North-Star Metric Proposal** | Proposed NSM with definition, instrumentation plan, dashboard design |
| **OKR Drafts** | Quarterly company and team-level OKRs with key results and owners |
| **Roadmap Doc** | Prioritized bets, rationale, confidence levels (using frameworks like ICE or confidence meter) |
| **Customer Interview Synthesis** | Patterns from 10–20 user interviews, mapped to opportunities |
| **AI Opportunity Map** | Ranked AI feature candidates with cost-justification, risk assessment, and "skip" recommendations |
| **Eval Harness Design** | Golden dataset spec, LLM-as-a-judge rubrics, regression suite design, tooling recommendation |
| **Prompt Versioning Workflow** | Workflow for prompt iteration independent of engineering deploys, using LangSmith / PromptLayer / Humanloop |
| **Inference Cost Model** | Per-feature inference cost at scale; routing logic (frontier model vs. small model) |
| **Succession Plan / Capability Brief** | Document that enables a future full-time PM to pick up where the fractional left off |

The Umbrex CPO Playbook specifies a 180-day delivery arc: spec templates and OKRs by Day 45; discovery repo and dashboards by Day 90; pricing experiments and NRR dashboards by Day 135; succession plan by Day 180.  
Source: [Umbrex — Fractional CPO Playbook](https://umbrex.com/resources/fractional-executive-playbook/fractional-chief-product-officer-playbook/)

Itamar Gilad's framing on "what counts as evidence" is useful for PRD/roadmap artifacts: show up not with a polished pitch deck but with real evidence and clear confidence levels. His GIST framework (Goals, Ideas, Steps, Tasks) emphasizes that process documentation is output, not outcome.  
Source: [Itamar Gilad — Three Common PM Role Antipatterns](https://itamargilad.com/pm-antipatterns/)

## 5. What Separates Great Fractional PMs from Mediocre Ones

This section pulls honest takes from named practitioners, not marketing copy.

---

### The core distinction: outcomes over hours

Nobody buys "two days a week." They buy what those two days unlock. If a CEO is pushing hard on price before they're clear on outcomes, it's usually a sign they're buying "hours," not change.

Source: [Prodify — How Can a Fractional Product Executive Help You?](https://www.prodify.group/blog/fractional-product-executives)

---

### John Cutler: The feature factory PM vs. the outcome-focused PM

Cutler's most cutting observation: "In 90% of teams, no one gets fired in the near/mid-term if they deliver a roadmap that everyone is bought into" — which is why feature factories persist. Mediocre fractional PMs slip into the same dynamic: they deliver roadmaps, run ceremonies, produce artifacts, and avoid conflict. A fractional PM who "succeeds" by making everyone comfortable without changing outcome trajectories is mediocre.

The top PMs, per Cutler, "work like sculptors, making time by paring away excesses." They protect the team's capacity for the right activities. The signal for a great fractional: they make the organization stronger so they become progressively less necessary.  
Source: [John Cutler — TBM 8/52: Top 1% Product Managers](https://cutlefish.substack.com/p/tbm-852-top-1-product-managers)

Also from Cutler on Lenny's Podcast: the highest-performing product teams share a few attributes — clarity on outcomes (not outputs), psychological safety for teams to push back, and a PM who is genuinely curious about the system.  
Source: [Lenny's Newsletter — What differentiates the highest-performing product teams | John Cutler](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-differentiates-the-highest-performing)

---

### Itamar Gilad: The Facilitator PM anti-pattern

"The Facilitator PM's job is to run between managers, stakeholders, and team, collect everyone's product ideas, then mediate some compromise. In practice good products are never 'discovered' through consensus, so this is a massive waste of PM talent."

And on process accumulation: "Managing the processes-of-the-day, ones they had no part in choosing, creates a ton of work for PMs. Accumulation of frameworks and methodologies can easily overwhelm and distract the product org... Ultimately — implementing a process is output, and one that doesn't necessarily lead to outcomes."  
Source: [Itamar Gilad — Three Common PM Role Antipatterns](https://itamargilad.com/pm-antipatterns/)

---

### Reforge: Judgment becomes more valuable as AI accelerates execution

"There has to be somebody who's bringing that intuition, the leadership, that judgment, the taste, and also the one who gets to decide... The faster your team can build, the more important it becomes that they're building the right things."

This is the core value proposition for a senior fractional PM in the AI era: the fractional PM's judgment and taste are precisely what can't be automated. As AI tools compress execution time, the scarce resource is high-quality strategic decisions.  
Source: [Reforge — Moving to Higher Ground: Product Management in the Age of AI](https://www.reforge.com/blog/ai-impact-product-management)

---

### Aakash Gupta: The honest picture of fractional work

From his 16,000-word guide (co-authored with Jason Knight of One Knight in Product), based on 20 interviews:

> "Fractional work comes with a constant hustle to keep the pipeline full, requiring fractional leaders to continuously network, market themselves, and seek out new opportunities — blending product management expertise, marketing savvy, and sales craft."

> "Fractional CPO was the hottest job in the product world as of May 2024."

What separates the great ones: those who diversify their client base across stages (pre-PMF, post-PMF, growth) build more sustainable practices. Those who take any client and overcommit on hours burn out and underdeliver.  
Source: [Aakash Gupta — Fractional Product Leadership: Your Step-By-Step Guide](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/fractional-product-leadership)

---

### Ben Erez: The pricing and anti-patterns

Ben Erez spent two years in fractional work and was Head of Product at Continuum (a marketplace for fractional executives). His LinkedIn post "Not sure how to price fractional product work?" addresses the common mistake of underpricing hourly.

His honest account includes "real revenue data showing the spiky, unsteady income reality of fractional work" — the income is not flat. Great fractional PMs plan for this with retainer-heavy portfolios and 2+ clients at any time.  
Source: [Ben Erez — #103: What senior product people should know about fractional work](https://suprainsider.substack.com/p/103-what-senior-product-people-should)

---

### Red flags in a fractional PM

From the Moonshot Partners and BPMA synthesis:
- Difficulty articulating approach or answering direct questions (communication failure early = friction late)
- Overpromising results or claiming expertise they haven't demonstrably worked in
- Resistance to feedback
- Treating the engagement as "hours sold" rather than outcomes owned
- Becoming the hero rather than building the organization's capability

## 7. AI/LLM PM Specifically

This is where the role has meaningfully differentiated from general fractional PM work, and where primary-source depth matters most.

---

### Why the AI PM role is different

Traditional PMs work with deterministic systems: you define a spec, engineering builds it, QA validates it, you ship it. LLM-powered features are **probabilistic**: identical inputs can produce different outputs. This breaks every traditional QA model and changes what a PM must own.

> "LLMs are probabilistic systems, meaning their outputs can vary even with identical inputs — a fundamental shift that requires rethinking quality assurance from the ground up."

Source: [PromptLayer — Product Manager Levels: LLM Competency](https://blog.promptlayer.com/product-manager-levels-llm-competency-the-new-rules-of-ai-product-management/)

---

### Evals: the defining skill for AI PMs in 2025

Aman Khan (Director of Product at Arize AI, ex-Spotify/Apple/Cruise), writing in Lenny's Newsletter (April 8, 2025):

> "Every PM building with generative AI obsesses over crafting better prompts and using the latest LLM, yet almost no one masters the hidden lever behind every exceptional AI product: evaluations."

> "The ability to write great evals isn't just important — it's rapidly becoming the defining skill for AI PMs in 2025 and beyond."

> "Evals are the only way you can break down each step in the system and measure specifically what impact an individual change might have on a product, giving you the data and confidence to take the right next step."

Source: [Lenny's Newsletter / Aman Khan — Beyond vibe checks: A PM's complete guide to evals](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/beyond-vibe-checks-a-pms-complete) (April 2025)

---

### What evals actually look like in practice

PMs validate prompts on **golden datasets** — curated test sets that mirror the product's edge cases, domains, and formats. This functions like a regression suite: you can quantify task success, spot regressions, and compare prompt or model changes with data rather than vibes.

**LLM-as-a-Judge**: Use an LLM to grade another LLM's outputs. Highly scalable, can generate explanations, and PMs can write the rubrics directly in natural language.

**Tracing / Observability**: Tools like Langfuse provide one platform to trace all LLM and non-LLM calls (retrieval, embedding, API calls), track multi-turn conversations, and run automated evaluations before releases.

> "Release to production, then use production evaluation / monitoring techniques to identify edge cases on which the application does not meet quality expectations."

Source: [Langfuse — LLM Product Development for Product Managers](https://langfuse.com/blog/2024-11-llm-product-management) (November 2024)

---

### Prompt versioning

PromptLayer and similar tools (LangSmith, Humanloop) log, track, and version every prompt and response. This lets PMs:
- Audit historical performance and spot regressions
- A/B test prompt variations systematically
- Decouple prompt iteration from engineering deployment cycles (domain experts and PMs can iterate independently)

> "Moving QA from a 'pass/fail' binary to systematic evaluation of how changes in prompt syntax impact output quality over time."

Source: [PromptLayer](https://blog.promptlayer.com/product-manager-levels-llm-competency-the-new-rules-of-ai-product-management/)

---

### Inference cost and the PM's cost ceiling responsibility

> "Every AI feature carries three cost layers: development, inference at scale, and retraining when the model drifts. Standard feature prioritization frameworks miss the second and third."

> "The AI feature roadmap that ignores inference cost looks clean in planning and becomes a $20,000 monthly surprise at 10 million calls."

The PM's cost governance job: set latency and token-cost budgets per feature and per flow; route high-frequency routine tasks to smaller, specialized models; reserve frontier model inference for high-margin, complex reasoning tasks.

Source: [AI Product Management guide — Patoliya Infotech](https://blog.patoliyainfotech.com/ai-product-management-guide/)

**Gartner projection (March 2026)**: Inference cost on a 1-trillion-parameter LLM will fall 90%+ by 2030. This means the cost governance decisions made now are temporary — but failing to make them in 2025–2026 can kill AI features before the economics improve.  
Source: [Gartner — March 2026 press release](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-03-25-gartner-predicts-that-by-2030-performing-inference-on-an-llm-with-1-trillion-parameters-will-cost-genai-providers-over-90-percent-less-than-in-2025)

---

### The "kill the AI feature" call

This is the decision nobody writes about explicitly. A PM with production LLM experience knows when to pull the plug:
- When retraining stops improving performance (the model has hit a ceiling for this data distribution)
- When the business problem has shifted and the training data no longer reflects reality
- When inference cost exceeds margin, even at optimized routing
- When regulation or compliance has shifted (GDPR, CCPA, emerging AI-specific laws) and the data pipeline is no longer compliant

Source: [AI PM role — Patoliya Infotech](https://blog.patoliyainfotech.com/ai-product-management-guide/), [PromptLayer LLM PM levels](https://blog.promptlayer.com/product-manager-levels-llm-competency-the-new-rules-of-ai-product-management/)

---

### Specifics on AI fractional PM positioning

The AI fractional PM niche is real and growing. Smith Horn Group explicitly positions for "AI-first software teams" and lists Boeing, Asana, and Cloudinary as clients. Their differentiator: "Our fractional leaders ship alongside your team, not from the sidelines."  
Source: [Smith Horn Group](https://www.smithhorn.ca/services/fractional-product-leadership)

From the fractional AI talent market perspective: "The core value proposition is getting the output of a senior AI hire — agents built, automations running, LLM features shipped — without the salary, benefits, hiring lag, or long-term commitment."  
Source: [Chrono Innovation — Why SaaS Companies Need Fractional AI](https://www.chronoinnovation.com/resources/why-saas-needs-fractional-ai)

Menlo Ventures' 2025 State of Generative AI report (surveying ~500 US enterprise decision-makers, released December 2025): enterprise AI investment tripled in one year to $37 billion; 76% of AI use cases are now *purchased* (vs. 47% built internally just a year prior). The demand for people who can evaluate, specify, and manage AI products — not just build them — is accelerating.  
Source: [Menlo Ventures — 2025: The State of Generative AI in the Enterprise](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/)

## Notes for Services Page Positioning

1. **The AI PM credential is the differentiator.** The gap between "has read about LLMs" and "has shipped LLM features to production and managed evals, prompt versioning, and cost governance" is real and wide. If Wesley has shipped AI features in production, that's the top-of-page claim.

2. **Be specific about engagement shapes and price them.** Published pricing anchors trust. Rohit Katiyar's tiered-by-ARR model is smart for startup buyers. Smith Horn's annual rate framing works for growth-stage buyers.

3. **The "fractional vs. consultant" distinction matters to buyers.** Make clear this is embedded execution with ownership, not advisory reports that sit on shelves.

4. **Parental leave / interim cover is an underrated niche.** It's a clean transaction: defined start, defined end, urgent need, easy-to-sell ROI. Worth listing even if it's not the primary focus.

5. **Reforge's framing is your best one-liner:** As AI accelerates execution, judgment becomes more valuable, not less. The scarce resource is the PM who can decide what the team should build.
